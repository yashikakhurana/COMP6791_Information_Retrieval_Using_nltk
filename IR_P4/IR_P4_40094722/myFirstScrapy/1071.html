Community Programs

Nutrition

Nutrition counselling programs are designed to provide clients with nutritional guidance and support. All nutritional evaluations and counselling sessions are conducted by a registered dietitian or a dietetic intern overseen by a registered dietitian

Gym Memberships

A membership in the PERFORM Gym allows you to benefit from the latest teaching methods, the research findings, and advanced fitness equipment which allows you to track your personal goals and progress. Participants are encouraged to take part in PERFORM's research and training programs.

Athletic Therapy Clinic

PERFORM's Athletic Therapy Clinic delivers prompt and professional care to staff and faculty, students as well as the general public. The Clinic is a fully functional treatment center with the very latest in equipment and modalities, including Swim-Ex for aqua therapy.

Parkinson's & Arthritis Wellness Programs

People Like Me are 12 week long wellness programs geared towards helping individuals with osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or Parkinson's. The programs include fitness training, physical therapy, and aqua therapy.

Group Classes

Join a group training class for fun, social workouts that are available at several levels of fitness & activity.

Contributing to advancements in health

The programs and services developed at PERFORM are based on expert research conducted on the best equipment available. Concordia students at all levels are contributors to the engagement of the community as well as the ongoing delivery of community programs and services. This is an extension of Concordia's longstanding commitment to the well-being of the community at large.

Benefits for the community

  • Participation in research projects

  • Opportunities to improve lifestyle and overall health

  • Access to the PERFORM Centre Gym, Athletic Therapy Clinic, Nutrition Services and Cardio-Pulmonary services.

Improving wellness

Community services at PERFORM are based on the latest research available. New services are often developed in response to lifestyle studies that have taken place at PERFORM and elsewhere. Other community based services are created in response to an needs within our local community or expressed by PERFORM researchers.  The ultimate objective is to ensure that new knowledge in preventive health is accessible to everybody in a practical way and can be taken on by other community service providers.
