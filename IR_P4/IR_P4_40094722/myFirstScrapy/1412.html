Web conference with Zoom

Zoom is a video conferencing tool that can­ be used in an academic setting to facilitate online learning. Faculty members can use it to provide lectures, tutorials and office hours. It is also helpful for online meetings, thesis defense, job interviews and guest speakers.

Staff may also use Zoom to host meetings but are recommended to use Teams since it is better integrated with administrative collaboration tools.

Concordia students also have access to a basic Zoom license with a time limit of 40 minutes to help facilitate their educational journey at Concordia.

IITS is also offering upon request, a webinar service which provide to Concordians the ability to hold an online seminar or other presentation, allowing participants in different locations to see and hear the presenter and ask questions through a Q&A chat.

If you would like assistance with recording a lecture on campus, please fill out the Class/laboratories onsite recording support form.

Please note: for information on how to use Zoom in an academic setting, see the tools and resources developed by the Centre for Teaching and Learning.

Available features:

Concordia’s Zoom licence:

  • 2 to 300 participants per web conferencing session with no time limit.
  • No time limit and additional educational features like breakout rooms, group chat, private chat, polling, screen-sharing, and live streaming on third party platforms.

Webinar event licence (available upon request):

  • Webinar events or classes for between 300 participants and 500 participants with no time limit.
  • For events/classes that needs more than 500 participants, we have few options available, please fill-in the request form and we will be in touch to assess your needs.
  • The webinar format is a one-way video and audio to the audience with chat-based communication (Q&A) for the participants.
  • More details about the difference between both options are listed in the FAQ section.

Who can use it?

Active faculty, staff and students can access Zoom Concordia through the Concordia web conferencing portal.   

How much does it cost?

There is no cost associated with this service.

Refer to the Equipment loan & rental page if on-site technical support or equipment loan is required.

Please note: During the COVID-19 pandemic, staff and faculty may also request the loan of a webcam, microphone, laptop and mobile internet connection device through this form.

How to get it

  • All active faculty, staff and students have access to a self-serve account online via the Concordia web conferencing portal by signing in with an active Concordia netname and password.

    • Temporary access to additional webinar sessions must be requested via the Technical assistance or Webinar license request form. Requests for additional features can be placed during Service Desk or Service Centre operating hours.
       
  • If you require additional on-site support or equipment, complete and submit the Technical assistance or Webinar license request form.

Service availability

24/7

Ensure Zoom is kept up-to-date

If you are working remotely, it is important to sign-on to Concordia University’s virtual private network (VPN) and update your computer and applications regularly. Doing so ensures that configuration changes can be applied and that you obtain important software updates and security patches as they become available.

For those using Zoom to deliver classes, hold meetings or undertake other remote conferencing activities, it is important to use the most recent Zoom version to benefit from the most recent security updates.

FAQ

What is a virtual meeting?

A virtual meeting is not a physical location - it is a communications solution that allows participants from multiple locations to connect video systems, desktop computers, tablets, and mobile devices in real time for the purpose of holding meetings, presentations, and collaboration sessions.

When to host a meeting versus when to host a webinar

Zoom meetings are ideal for hosting more interactive sessions where you'll want to have lots of audience participation or break your session into smaller groups.  Whereas, webinars may be considered like a virtual hall or auditorium.  Webinars are ideal for large audiences or events that are open to the public.  Typically webinar attendees do not interact with one another.  Though Zoom provides options for you to get more social with your attendees, your average webinar has one or a few people speaking to an audience.

What is the service offering for the webinar by IITS

IITS has made the acquisition of a pool of Webinar licences centrally managed by IITS staff to avoid being used for concurrent events. IITS staff offers support to proficiently customize the webinar services upon request for Concordians. Licensed Zoom accounts have been made available to Concordians to independently conduct their own web conference or online meeting at their convenience. IITS technicians can provide first-time users with consultation and walk-throughs of the Zoom platform in order to help be better prepared to use the tool.

IITS is offering a Zoom webinar service which provides Concordian faculty and staff members with the ability to hold an online seminar or other presentation, allowing participants in different locations to see and hear the presenter and ask questions through a Q&A interface and chat. 

IITS is offering online technical consultation to help with the setup of the events, technical support and live support alongside the events as well as attendance reports after the event. 

IITS is providing this service to support faculty and staff members with the technical aspects of delivering a webinar. We can offer and schedule a walk-through of the platform prior to your scheduled event in order to help you be better prepared to use the tool. 

We recommend that the Host or Organizer have a facilitator participate in the webinar to follow and moderate the Q&A dashboard, facilitate the flow of the webinar and energize interactions throughout the event. 

IITS is not offering a masters of ceremony or facilitator service as part of the webinar services we offer.

IITS is not offering web, video or audio production services nor branding/logo creation services. 

All media required within the scope of the webinar should be provided, prepared and controlled (played) by the Host or Organizer of the webinar, or their assigned facilitator. 

If you are using or planning to use the webinar service as part of an onsite or virtual event and you are already working with Hospitality Concordia, please reach out to hospitality@concordia.ca to ensure that our efforts are best coordinated.

Is Zoom secure?

Yes, the web conference service provides multi-layer protection which includes secure data centers, infrastructure, media handling & encryption, and application-level security. Please refer to the best practices for securing Zoom meetings [212 KB] document for more information on how to use Zoom securely.

What are the minimum requirements to use this system?

The minimum requirements for a device to use this system are:

  • An internet connection (wired, wireless, 3G, or 4G/LTE)
  • Speakers and microphone if audio is being used
  • A webcam or HD webcam if video is being used

Supported operating systems:

  • Mac OS X with MacOS 10.7 or later
  • Mac OS 10.6.8 (Snow Leopard) with limited functionality
  • Windows 10
  • Windows 8 or 8.1
  • Windows 7
  • Windows Vista with SP1 or later

Supported tablets and mobile devices:

  • Surface PRO 2 running Win 8.1
  • Surface PRO 3 running Win 10
  • iOS and Android devices
  • BlackBerry devices

Supported browsers:

  • Windows: IE7+, Firefox, Chrome, and Safari5+
  • Mac: Safari5+, Firefox, Chrome
  • Linux: Firefox, Chrome

How do I access my web conference account?

You can access your basic account by signing into the Concordia web conferencing portal using your Concordia netname and password. The account allows for an unlimited number of web conferencing sessions for an unlimited time with up to 300 participants.

I am a student how can I create a Zoom account?

In order to acquire a Zoom account, students must:

1- Create an Office 365 account.

2- After creating an Office 365 account, students must update their preferred email address in My Student Centre using their Concordia address.

3- Once completed, students can access Zoom Concordia through the Concordia Zoom web conferencing portal.

How do I get started with hosting or joining a meeting?

  1. Sign into your account at https://concordia-ca.zoom.us using your Concordia netname and password.
  2. Download the client (available for PC, MAC, and mobile devices) from https://concordia-ca.zoom.us/download.
  3. Launch the downloaded application.
  4. Choose the function that best fits your needs - join a meeting (to access a meeting that is in progress) or sign in (to schedule or host a meeting).

Mobile users can download the mobile application directly to their device by visiting the app store (iTunes, Google Play Store, etc.) and searching for the 'Zoom' application.

If you are using Outlook, you can download an add-on to easily create your virtual meeting:https://zoom.us/client/latest/ZoomOutlookPluginSetup.msi

I am unable to hear my participants. What do I do?

By default, all meeting participants are muted when they initially join the meeting.
The meeting host has the ability to mute/unmute participants manually or change meeting settings to allow participants to unmute themselves.

What is a meeting host?

The meeting host is often referred to as the “chair”. The meeting host has overall control, including the ability to mute/unmute other participants and disconnect other users.
